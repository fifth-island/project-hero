Critical Research Insight

Humidity acts as the primary driver of physiological heat stress in Chinatown’s microclimates. While dry temperature varies minimally, moisture retention creates a 47% dual-exposure risk where peak heat coincides with high particulate matter.

Key Action Item

Prioritize green infrastructure at Tufts Medical & Tai Tung to mitigate the "Night-Heat Retention" cycle.
