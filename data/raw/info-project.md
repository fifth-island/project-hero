Datasets attached:
• Environmental conditions ("data_HEROS.xlsx"): PM2.5 from Purple Air sensors
installed at 12 open space sites, heat stress indicators from Kestrel sensors
installed at 12 open space sites; meteorological data from weather station monitor
on top of 35 Kneeland Street; PM2.5 from MassDEP FEM from their Chinatown
location (i.e., brick building next to Reggie Wong Park in Chinatown); and PM2.5 and
related data from the MassDEP FEM data from Nubian Square (their Roxbury
location).
• Codebook ("codebook_HEROS.xlsx")
• Landuse data and codebook ("landuse_HEROS.xlsx"): Information extracted from
public records. Data represents the percentages of green space, roads, impervious
surfaces, and industrial buildings estimated at 25-meter and 50-meet circular
buffers at each site. Data and codebook in different tabs
Some questions that students can answer in visual and quantitative ways:
1. How do Purple Air PM2.5 data at each of the 12 open space sites in Chinatown
compare with the Mass DEP FEM PM2.5 data in Chinatown and Nubian Square?
2. How Kestrel ambient temperature data at each of the 12 open space site in
Chinatown compare with the ambient temperature data from the weather station at
35 Kneeland St and from the Mass DEP FEM monitor at Nubian Square?
3. Create separate cumulative distribution plots (CDF) of PM2.5 and wet bulb glob
temperature overall, by time of day (e.g. daytime, nighttime), and for each of the 12
open space sites, Chinatown DEP site, and Nubian Square site.
4. What were the air quality index and concentrations of other pollutants (CO, SO2,
NO2, Ozone) in Chinatown between July 19 - August 2023 based on the MassDEP
monitor? Extract data from website and merge with "data_HEROS" data:
https://eeaonline.eea.state.ma.us/dep/massair/web/#/pollution/map/max
5. Pick the hottest days for summer of 2023 and visualize potential differences in wet
bulb globe temperature across sites
6. Pick the highest AQI (Air Quality Index) days for summer of 2023 and visualize
potential differences in PM2.5 across sites
7. What is the relationship between PM2.5 and heat indicators, controlling for time of
day, day of week, wind speed, relative humidity, and wind direction? Another other
factors? Is there heterogeneity across sites?
8. What time(s) of day and day(s) of week are associated with the highest wet bulb
globe temperatures and PM2.5 overall and for each open space site?
9. Look at the landuse buffer characteristics across sites and whether they are
associated with PM2.5 and heat indicators
